IEEE ITSC 2025 Workshop on Transportation 5.0

Towards Cyber-Physical-Social Intelligent Transportation Systems

About the Workshop

With the rapid advancement and integration of cutting-edge technologies like IoT, edge computing, blockchain, and generative AI, massive amounts of real-time social and physical data are being generated for transportation systems. Transportation 5.0, the next evolution in intelligent mobility, is built on Cyber-Physical-Social Systems (CPSS), enabling deeper human-machine collaboration. As one of the most complex engineered systems, transportation faces multifaceted challenges that demand cost-effective, time-sensitive, and spatially optimized solutions.

This workshop aims to inspire innovative approaches to these challenges by leveraging CPSS-based Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS), which harness real-time social and physical data for smarter decision-making. The focus is on applying CPSS-empowered ITS to enhance traffic planning, dynamic routing, adaptive management, predictive control, and data-driven transportation analytics--delivering greater efficiency, precision, agility, and scalability. We invite researchers and industry leaders to explore this transformative frontier in ITS and shape the future of mobility.

Topics of Interest

  • Cyber-Physical-Social Systems (CPSS) for transportation
  • Parallel transportation with knowledge automation
  • Big Models and Foundation Intelligence for ITS
  • GenAI and AGI for integrated and sustainable transportation system
  • Decentralized Autonomous Organizations and Operations (DAOs) for ITS
  • Crowdsourcing mechanisms for transportation-based Internet of Everything
  • New and smart ITS services beyond location-based services (LBS): decision-based services (DBS), task-based services (TBS), information or intelligence-based services (IBS) or knowledge-based services (KBS)
  • ITS with Mobile Internet, Cloud Computing, and Internet of Things, Services, and Everything
Keywords: Cyber-Physical-Social Systems, Parallel Transportation, Computational Transportation Experiments, Decentralized Autonomous Organizations and Operations
